Mach number relates to the speed of speed of the aircraft with the speed of sound. The presence of an aircraft is felt through the pressure waves generated by the aircraft as it flies through the atmosphere. For any sound propagation we need (medium) pressure (classic bell-jar experiment), in other words the speed at which the pressure waves travels is nothing but the sound speed. Mach number tells if the aircraft is leading or lagging behind the pressure waves.

Knudsen number relates to the mean distances traveled by the molecules/atoms to the characteristic length of the aircraft (length). At the edge of the atmosphere, the pressure is negligibly small and the air molecules have to travel long distances to collide with another air molecule. In other words, this travel distance for collisions is of the same order of magnitude as the characteristic length of the aircraft.

Commercial airlines cruise at ~10 km altitude, near the edge of Troposphere layer. Temperature varies linearly in the Troposphere layer and it remains constant (around -50 degrees Celsius) in the Tropopause layer which stretches from ~ 10-20 km. Using sea level conditions one could easily calculate the atmospheric conditions for up to 20 km.

 

Next time when you take a flight (when you run out of all movie playlist), these values can be handy to calculate the flight speeds, Reynolds number, Mach number in real time! This is how aero folks disseminate aerodynamics knowledge to other passengers onboard :)
